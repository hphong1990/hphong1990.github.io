About

I am a faculty member at the Faculty of Mechanical Engineering and Mechatronics, Phenikaa University, Hanoi. My research sits at the intersection of scientific machine learning, computational physics, and intelligent robotics — building models that learn from data while respecting the governing laws of the physical world.

My work has evolved from 3D geometric understanding and computational design toward physics-informed neural networks and dynamical systems modeling. The common thread: using mathematical structure to make machine learning work reliably in physical domains where data is scarce and mistakes are costly. My research has been published in venues including ICML, Science Advances, Scientific Reports, Additive Manufacturing, and Automation in Construction.

I collaborate closely with researchers at the University of Virginia, University of Iowa, Columbia University, and Kyungpook National University.

Research

Three threads, one question:
how do physical systems learn?

01

Physics-Informed Machine Learning

Neural networks trained not only on data but constrained by the governing equations of physics — PDEs, conservation laws, dynamical system structure. This makes learning reliable in sparse-data regimes where purely data-driven methods fail. Core work includes PARCv2 (ICML 2024) and microstructure synthesis using GANs and reinforcement learning.

02

Geometric Intelligence & 3D Vision

Computational understanding of 3D geometry — point cloud processing, spatial object recognition, and geometric reasoning for industrial and robotic applications. This thread connects classical CAD/CAM to modern deep learning, enabling machines to see and reason about the shape of the physical world.

03

Data-Driven Design & Manufacturing

Accelerating engineering design through machine learning surrogates — topology optimization, functionally graded structures, additive manufacturing. The goal: designs that are not only optimal but buildable, connecting the computational and physical worlds across the full design-to-fabrication pipeline.

04

Intelligent Physical Systems

The forward frontier: robots and autonomous systems that perceive, model, and act in dynamic physical environments. Drawing on physics-informed learning and 3D perception to build systems capable of human-robot interaction grounded in physical awareness — where scientific ML meets embodied intelligence.
